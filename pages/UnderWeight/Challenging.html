Excercise Regimen

Strength Training

Frequency: 5-6 sessions per week
Rest: Allow at least one rest day between sessions

Day 1: Upper Body:

Bench Press: 5 sets of 6-8 reps
Pull-Ups: 5 sets to failure
Overhead Press: 5 sets of 6-8 reps
Dumbbell Rows: 5 sets of 8-10 reps
Tricep Dips: 4 sets to failure

Day 2: Lower Body:

Squats: 5 sets of 6-8 reps
Deadlifts: 5 sets of 6-8 reps
Lunges: 4 sets of 10 reps (each leg)
Leg Press: 4 sets of 10 reps

Day 3: Rest

Day 4: Full Body:

Deadlifts: 5 sets of 6-8 reps
Bench Press: 5 sets of 6-8 reps
Pull-Ups: 5 sets to failure
Leg Press: 4 sets of 10 reps
Overhead Press: 4 sets of 8-10 reps

Day 5: Rest

Day 6: High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT):

Sprint intervals: 30 seconds sprint, 1 minute rest, repeat for 25 minutes
Burpees: 4 sets of 10-15 reps
Jumping Lunges: 4 sets of 10-15 reps

Day 7: Rest or Light Activity

Additional Tips

Hydration:

Drink water throughout the day, aiming for at least 8 glasses. Include other hydrating options like coconut water or fresh fruit juices.

Meal Preparation:

Plan meals ahead of time to ensure balanced nutrition. Keep healthy snacks readily available for convenience.

Sleep:

Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep each night to support overall well-being and recovery.

Track Progress:

Regularly assess weight, strength gains, and overall progress to make necessary adjustments to the diet and exercise routine.

Supplements:

Consider adding a protein powder to smoothies to help meet daily protein goals.Multivitamins and fish oil supplements can support overall health.

Mindset:

Stay committed to the challenging journey of gaining muscle mass. Embrace the intensity of workouts and the discipline required for nutrition. Celebrate achievements and milestones along the way.
