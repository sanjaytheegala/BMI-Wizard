Excercise Regimen

Cardiovascular Exercise:

Brisk Walking: Start with 10-15 minutes of brisk walking, gradually increasing to 30-45 minutes as endurance improves. Aim for a pace that elevates your heart rate and makes you slightly breathless but still able to carry on a conversation.
Water Aerobics: Consider joining a water aerobics class at your local pool. Water provides resistance while being gentle on the joints, making it an excellent option for those with obesity.

Strength Training

Activities:
Bodyweight Exercises: Perform bodyweight exercises targeting major muscle groups. Start with 2-3 sets of 8-12 repetitions for each exercise, gradually increasing intensity and repetitions as you build strength.
Include exercises such as:
Squats: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, then return to the starting position.
Wall Push-Ups: Stand facing a wall, place your hands on the wall at shoulder height, and perform push-ups by bending your elbows and lowering your chest towards the wall.
Seated Leg Lifts: Sit on a chair with your back straight, lift one leg straight out in front of you, hold for a few seconds, then lower it back down. Alternate legs.
Resistance Bands: Incorporate resistance bands for added resistance and variety in your strength training routine. Perform exercises such as bicep curls, lateral raises, and seated rows.

Flexibility and Mobility:

Stretching: Dedicate time to stretching exercises to improve flexibility and mobility. Hold each stretch for 15-30 seconds and repeat 2-3 times. Focus on stretching major muscle groups such as the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, chest, back, and shoulders.
Yoga or Tai Chi: Consider joining a beginner-level yoga or Tai Chi class to improve flexibility, balance, and relaxation. These practices also promote mindfulness and body awareness.

Additional Tips

Hydration:

Drink water before meals to help control hunger and avoid overeating. Aim for at least 8-10 cups of water per day.Avoid sugary drinks and excessive caffeine.

Portion Control:

Use smaller plates and bowls to help control portion sizes and prevent overeating.

Progressive Overload:

As your fitness improves, gradually increase the duration, intensity, and frequency of your workouts. This principle of progressive overload helps stimulate muscle growth and cardiovascular adaptation over time.Keep track of your progress by logging your workouts and gradually challenging yourself to do more each week.

Avoid Packaged Foods:

Minimize consumption of packaged and processed foods as they often contain hidden sugars, unhealthy fats, and additives that can hinder weight loss progress. Opt for whole, natural foods whenever possible.

Support System

Join a weight loss group or community for motivation and accountability. A support network can provide encouragement and help you stay on track.

Safety Precautions:

Listen to your body and avoid pushing yourself too hard, especially in the beginning. If you experience pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath during exercise, stop immediately and seek medical advice if needed.Stay hydrated before, during, and after exercise, especially if you're sweating heavily.Wear comfortable, supportive footwear and clothing that allows for freedom of movement.

Patience and Persistence

Understand that significant weight loss takes time and consistent effort. Stay committed to your exercise program and lifestyle changes, and celebrate progress along the way.

Activity Increase

Incorporate more physical activities into daily routines, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator.
