Excercise Regimen

Cardio

Frequency: 2-3 times per week
Duration: Begin with 10-15 minutes per session, aiming to work up to 30 minutes
Activities:
Seated Cardio: Utilize recumbent bikes or seated elliptical machines to minimize stress on joints while still providing effective cardiovascular exercise.
Water Aerobics: Join a water aerobics class or perform gentle water exercises in a pool to reduce impact on joints and improve cardiovascular health.

Strength Training

Frequency: Start with 1-2 times per week, gradually increasing as strength and endurance improve
Activities:
Chair Exercises: Perform seated leg lifts, arm curls with light weights, and seated torso twists using a sturdy chair for support.
Resistance Bands: Incorporate resistance band exercises such as seated rows, chest presses, and leg extensions to build muscle strength without heavy weights.

Additional Tips

Hydration:

Drink water before meals to help control hunger and avoid overeating. Aim for at least 8-10 cups of water per day.Avoid sugary drinks and excessive caffeine.

Portion Control:

Use smaller plates and bowls to help control portion sizes and prevent overeating.

Activity Increase:

Incorporate more physical activities into daily routines, such as walking instead of driving or taking stairs instead of elevators.

Avoid Packaged Foods:

Minimize consumption of packaged and processed foods as they often contain hidden sugars, unhealthy fats, and additives that can hinder weight loss progress. Opt for whole, natural foods whenever possible.

Support System

Join a weight loss group or community for motivation and accountability. A support network can provide encouragement and help you stay on track.

Consistency

Aim for consistency in your exercise routine, gradually increasing frequency, duration, and intensity over time to achieve long-term success.

Patience and Persistence

Understand that significant weight loss takes time and consistent effort. Stay committed to your exercise program and lifestyle changes, and celebrate progress along the way.

Gradual Progression

Start with low-intensity exercises and gradually increase intensity and duration as fitness improves. Listen to your body and avoid pushing too hard too soon to prevent injury.
